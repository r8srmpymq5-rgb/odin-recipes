Mojito

The Carribean Classic.

The Mojito, while possessing deep historical roots in Cuban culture, has been refined and standardized over time, with its perfect modern § presentation often attributed to Caribbean resorts and bars. Most sources agree that the Mojito formula solidified in the early-to-mid 20th century, making it a quintessential tropical cocktail. Unlike heavier, spirit-forward drinks, it is an exemplary example of a "high-acid, aromatic" cocktail, defined by the bright contrast of lime and mint. It requires a unique preparation—muddling—to release the volatile oils, which are crucial to its profile. It quickly became a signature drink of Caribbean and coastal locales, gaining popularity as the quintessential refreshment for hot weather. Its immediate, bracing flavor and vibrant, fizzy lift cemented its status as an indispensable "summer starter" and staple of any tropical gathering.

Ingredients.

  • 10-12 Fresh Mint Leaves
  • 1 Lime (cut into 4 wedges)
  • 2 teaspoons Simple Syrup (or sugar)
  • 2 oz White Rum
  • 3-4 oz Club Soda (Soda Water)
  • Ice cubes (for serving)
  • Mint sprig or lime wheel (for garnish)

Method.

  1. Place the mint leaves, lime wedges, and simple syrup into the bottom of a highball glass.
  2. Gently muddle (press and twist) the ingredients a few times to release the oils from the mint and juice from the lime.
  3. Pour the white rum into the glass.
  4. Fill the glass about halfway with ice cubes.
  5. Top off the mixture with club soda.
  6. Stir gently to combine all the ingredients.
  7. Garnish with a fresh mint sprig and serve immediately.
