SINGAPORE SLING

A Tropical Icon with a Storied Past

The Singapore Sling is an icon of Southeast Asian cocktail culture, with roots stretching back to the early 20th century. It was famously popularized at the Long Bar in the Raffles Hotel in Singapore. While the exact date of its invention is unclear, it became a staple of colonial-era high society. The drink was designed to be a sophisticated alternative to heavier spirits, offering a lighter, fruitier option for women and travelers in the tropical heat. It remains one of the most famous "destination cocktails" in the world, synonymous with the glamour of old Singapore.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz gin
  • 1 oz cherry brandy
  • 1 oz triple sec
  • 1 oz fresh lime juice
  • 1 tsp grenadine
  • 1 cup ice

Steps

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
  2. Add gin, cherry brandy, triple sec, fresh lime juice, and grenadine to the shaker.
  3. Shake vigorously for about 15 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients.
  4. Strain the mixture into a tall glass filled with ice.
  5. Garnish with a cherry and a sprig of mint if desired.
