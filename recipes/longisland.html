LONG ISLAND ICE TEA

The Classic 'Party Starter'

The Long Island Ice Tea is a relatively modern creation, though its exact "birth" is a subject of much debate among bartenders. Most historians agree it emerged in the United States during the late 1970s, likely in Long Island, New York, though some claim it originated in California. Unlike traditional iced teas, it is a "spirit-forward" cocktail designed to deliver a high alcohol content in a deceptively refreshing, tea-like package. It became a cultural phenomenon in bars and clubs throughout the 1980s and 90s, known for its reputation as a "party starter" due to its potency.

Ingredients.

  • 1 oz vodka
  • 1 oz tequila
  • 1 oz rum
  • 1 oz triple sec
  • 1 oz lemon juice
  • 1 tsp simple syrup
  • 1 cup ice

Method.

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice.
  2. Add vodka, tequila, rum, triple sec, lemon juice, and simple syrup to the shaker.
  3. Shake vigorously for about 15 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients.
  4. Strain the mixture into a tall glass filled with ice.
  5. Top off with a splash of cola for color and a hint of sweetness.
  6. Garnish with a lemon wedge or a sprig of mint if desired.

Enjoy responsibly if you can. (We know you can't!)
